NCERT Solutions Class 10 Maths Chapter 6: Triangles

Detailed solutions for Exercise 6.1 (Introduction to Similarity).

Ex 6.1 Ex 6.2 Ex 6.3
#### **Q1. Fill in the blanks using the correct word given in brackets:** **(i) All circles are \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_. (congruent, similar)** **(ii) All squares are \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_. (similar, congruent)** **(iii) All \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_ triangles are similar. (isosceles, equilateral)** **(iv) Two polygons of the same number of sides are similar, if (a) their corresponding angles are \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_ and (b) their corresponding sides are \_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_\_. (equal, proportional)** #### **A1. Solution:** (i) All circles are **similar**. (Reason: They have the same shape but can have different radii). (ii) All squares are **similar**. (Reason: All angles are $90^\circ$ and sides are always proportional). (iii) All **equilateral** triangles are similar. (Reason: Each angle is always $60^\circ$). (iv) Two polygons of the same number of sides are similar, if (a) their corresponding angles are **equal** and (b) their corresponding sides are **proportional**. #### **Q2. Give two different examples of a pair of:** **(i) similar figures.** **(ii) non-similar figures.** #### **A2. Solution:** **(i) Similar figures:** 1. Any two circles (e.g., a circle of radius 2 cm and a circle of radius 5 cm). 2. Any two equilateral triangles (e.g., one with side 3 cm and another with side 6 cm). **(ii) Non-similar figures:** 1. A circle and a triangle. 2. A scalene triangle and an equilateral triangle. #### **Q3. State whether the following quadrilaterals are similar or not:** *(Refer to the image in the textbook: A rhombus PQRS with sides 1.5 cm and a square ABCD with sides 3 cm)* #### **A3. Solution:** The two quadrilaterals are **not similar**. **Reasoning:** 1. **Sides:** The ratio of the corresponding sides is constant: $\frac{1.5}{3} = \frac{1}{2}$. The sides are proportional. 2. **Angles:** In square ABCD, all angles are $90^\circ$. However, in rhombus PQRS, the angles are not $90^\circ$. Since the corresponding angles are not equal, the two quadrilaterals are not similar.
